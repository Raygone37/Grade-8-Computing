Hello teachers and students.
This page will be for the student regulatins and obligations.

Rights:

  1. As a student, I have the right to join academic and non-academic activities, whether online or onsite. For me, this means I can learn not only in class but also outside class through clubs and events. For example, I can participate in a basketball competition or an online seminar. I will make the effort to join at least one activity that helps me grow.
  2. I have the right to receive religious education according to my Christian faith. To me, this means I can grow spiritually through devotions and Bible study in school. For example, attending chapel services helps me strengthen my relationship with God. I will always bring my Bible and be active in worship.
  3. I have the right to use school facilities properly. It means I can benefit from the library, lab, and sports hall, as long as I respect the rules. For example, I can borrow books and return them on time. I will always use facilities responsibly so others can also enjoy them.
  4. I have the right to receive equal treatment from teachers, principals, and staff. For me, this means I should be treated fairly no matter my background. For example, if I have a problem, I deserve to be listened to respectfully. I will also treat others fairly, because fairness goes both ways.
  5. I have the right to access guidance and counseling. That means if I feel stressed or confused, I can seek help. For example, I can talk to a school counselor when I struggle with studies. I will not be afraid to ask for help when I need it.
  6. I have the right to receive health services from the school clinic. To me, this means if I'm unwell or injured, I can get help. For example, when I had a fever, I could rest at the clinic until my parents came. I will always take care of my health and use the clinic wisely.
  7. I have the right to defend myself against accusations. For me, this means I can explain my side before receiving punishment. For example, if I'm accused of cheating, I can show my notes or drafts as proof. I will always be honest and responsible so my defense is strong.
  8. I have the right to develop myself through extracurricular activities. It means I can join clubs that grow my talents. For example, joining band improves my musical skills. I will take part in at least one activity that builds my future.
  9. I have the right to take remedial or make-up assessments if needed. For me, this means I get a second chance when I fail or miss a test. For example, if I was sick, I can take a make-up exam. I will always use this right responsibly and study harder.
  10. I have the right to receive feedback on assignments within two weeks. It means I deserve timely comments to improve. For example, getting my essay back with feedback helps me know my mistakes. I will always reflect on feedback and do better next time.
  11. I have the right to submit suggestions to school leaders. For me, this means I can share constructive ideas politely. For example, I can suggest a recycling program through the student council. I will always give ideas that help the community, not just myself.
  12. I have the right to join competitions with principal approval. It means I can represent my school in events. For example, I can compete in a science fair after selection. I will always do my best to make my school proud.
  13. I have the right to get information and join selections for scholarships or exchanges. For me, this means opportunities are open to all who qualify. For example, applying for a student exchange can help me grow internationally. I will check announcements and apply seriously.
  14. I have the right to receive my report card or diploma if all requirements are complete. It means I need to finish my obligations first. For example, I must return library books before getting my diploma. I will make sure to clear all responsibilities on time.
  15. I have the right to receive my report card or diploma if I withdraw or graduate, provided my obligations are done. For me, this means completing clearance is necessary. For example, students leaving must settle fees and return school property. I will respect this rule and finish what is required.

Obligations:

  • I must uphold the school's core values: Love, Joy, Peace, Patience, Kindness, Goodness, Faithfulness, Gentleness, and Self-control. For me, this means living like Christ in daily life. For example, showing kindness to classmates. I will practice at least one of these values each day.
  • I must follow all school rules. It means I respect both written and spoken instructions. For example, submitting homework on time. I will obey rules because they shape my character.
  • I must respect all school members. For me, this means speaking politely and acting courteously. For example, greeting my teachers every morning. I will treat everyone with respect, young or old.
  • I must protect the school's good name. It means I will act responsibly on campus and online. For example, not posting harmful content about the school. I will always represent my school positively.
  • I must arrive at school on time. For me, this means being punctual and disciplined. For example, being seated before class starts. I will set my alarm earlier to avoid being late.
  • I must participate in all classes and school programs. It means I can't skip what is required. For example, joining assemblies and special days. I will take part actively in every program.
  • I must inform and ask permission when absent. For me, this means being responsible for attendance. For example, sending a doctor's note if sick. I will always inform my teacher properly if I cannot attend.
  • I must ask questions when I don't understand. It means I shouldn't stay silent when confused. For example, asking my math teacher to re-explain a step. I will be brave to ask for help when I need it.
  • I must bring the required books and tools. For me, this means being prepared every day. For example, bringing a calculator to math class. I will always pack my bag the night before.
  • I must participate in school events and activities. It means I should be involved in the community. For example, joining a class performance in a talent show. I will contribute when asked, not just watch.
  • I must keep the classroom clean and orderly. For me, this means respecting shared space. For example, throwing trash in the bin. I will help make the class a comfortable place for everyone.
  • I am responsible for school inventory. It means if I damage something through negligence, I replace it. For example, reporting a broken chair immediately. I will always take care of school property.
  • I must take care of my belongings and follow phone rules. For me, this means submitting my phone to my homeroom teacher. For example, placing my phone in the box during devotion. I will always follow the rule with honesty.
  • I must dress neatly and modestly according to rules. It means wearing the right uniform on the right day. For example, batik on Friday and PE uniform during PE class. I will always respect the dress code.
  • I must follow grooming standards. For me, as a male, that means neat short hair and no dye. For example, I should not come with colored or messy hair. I will maintain proper grooming to show discipline.
  • I must resolve problems through school procedures. It means following the right channels. For example, reporting issues to my mentor before going higher. I will respect the process and not act recklessly.
  • I must accept consequences if I break rules. For me, this means learning from discipline. For example, doing guidance sessions when required. I will take correction seriously to grow better.
  • I must pay school tuition on time. It means fulfilling financial responsibilities. For example, preparing fees before the due date. I will always remind my parents about deadlines early.
  • I must request permission to use school facilities after hours. For me, this means respecting school property and schedules. For example, booking the sports hall properly. I will always ask before using facilities.
  • I must use social media responsibly. It means avoiding hate, bullying, or offensive posts. For example, posting encouragement instead of insults. I will use my social media to build others up.
